My Story

Today I'm a ux designer passionate about researching and creating accessible solutions for hardware and physical products. I actually started my journey in Mechanical Engineering. I thought engineering was a good fit for two reasons:

1. My Dad is a successful engineer.

2. I played with legos as a kid.

My Dad and I after finishing re-wiring my 1979 Ford Bronco.

After four semesters and two internships I decided that Mechanical Engineering was not the right fit for me. I was more inspired by the story behind a product, and the design, rather than maximizing efficiency or lowering manufacturing costs.

Presenting mockups for Helping Home application with my Teach Access team.

As soon as I started pursuing a degree in UX, I fell in love with the incredible influence a human-centered approach has on improving every aspect of a product. More importanly, I learned about accessiblity, usability and how rewarding and meaningful a career in Accessiblity can be.

Trying out the Magic Leap One at the 2019 XR Symposium

Today I do my best to approach problems with curiosity, empathy, and a supportive attitude. I also work well within cross-functional teams thanks to my wide range of experience and interests!
